# Sidequest Prototyping

Sidequest prototyping is a product-discovery pattern where small, low-permission experiments are built quickly and judged by whether people actually keep using them.

The pattern:

1. Build a rough prototype quickly.
2. Put it in front of real internal or external users.
3. Watch whether they return to it without persuasion.
4. Polish and productize what gets repeated use.
5. Let unused experiments die without ceremony.

This is valuable because it replaces speculative planning with behavior. A prototype that people keep using is stronger evidence than a roadmap argument.

The concept should not become an excuse for random motion. Sidequests are useful when the team can cheaply test a hunch and when adoption is visible. They become wasteful when no one records what was tried, why it mattered, or what happened.

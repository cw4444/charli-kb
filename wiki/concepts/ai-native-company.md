# AI Native Company

An AI native company is designed around AI as an operating layer, not merely as a productivity tool added to existing workflows.

The key shift is from "use AI to do the same work faster" to "redesign the company so important work is captured, queryable, and improvable by agents."

Practical traits:

- Meetings, support requests, product decisions, engineering work, and customer feedback produce durable artifacts.
- Agents can inspect those artifacts and answer operational questions.
- Dashboards expose current state rather than requiring status theater.
- Specs, tests, and success criteria become more important because agents can implement against them.
- Humans focus on judgment, customer outcomes, constraints, and taste.

This concept overlaps with [Agent Friendly Repositories](agent-friendly-repositories.md). A repo is one small version of an AI native organization: it should be legible enough that an agent can understand what matters, act safely, and improve the system.

# Identity Change As Goal Reprogramming

The batch frames identity as a goal-protection system. A person does not merely fail to act; they often act in service of hidden goals such as safety, belonging, predictability, avoidance of judgment, or preservation of an old self-image.

The useful model:

- Behavior reveals goals more honestly than stated intentions.
- Identity defends itself when a new goal threatens old patterns.
- Change becomes easier when the old pattern feels more threatening than the new effort.
- Environment, inputs, social context, and daily routines can reinforce either the old identity or the new one.
- The gap between impulse and response is where a person can interrupt the old pattern.

This is useful as a lens, but it should not be treated as proven psychology without better sources. It belongs in the wiki as a practical self-reflection model, not as a scientific claim.

# Focus Through Goal Structure

Focus is treated here as an outcome of clear goal structure, not raw willpower. The repeated claim across the source batch is that attention stabilizes when a person knows what matters, why it matters, what is at stake, and what the next concrete action is.

The pattern:

1. Define an anti-vision: the future to avoid.
2. Define a vision: the future to move toward.
3. Translate the vision into a one-year direction.
4. Translate that into a one-month project.
5. Translate the project into daily lever-moving tasks.
6. Protect the task block from distractions and competing identities.

The practical value is the hierarchy. Large goals provide direction, but small goals provide clarity. A person does not need endless motivation when the current task is specific enough to start and meaningful enough to care about.

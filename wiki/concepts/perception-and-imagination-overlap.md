# Perception And Imagination Overlap

Perception and imagination appear to use partly overlapping visual systems. This creates a puzzle: if imagined and perceived images share machinery, why does a unicorn imagined on the street not normally feel as real as the street itself?

The reality-threshold model answers by treating imagination and perception as contributing to a shared subjective signal. The brain does not merely ask "did I intend to imagine this?" It evaluates the strength and vividness of the experience. If that signal is below threshold, it is treated as imagined. If it is above threshold, it may be treated as real.

The 2025 follow-up work points to two candidate regions:

- Fusiform gyrus: tracks visual vividness or a reality signal.
- Anterior insula: becomes more active when an experience is judged real.

This is still early evidence. The safest use of the concept is as a model for reality monitoring, not as a complete account of hallucination, dreaming, or psychosis.
